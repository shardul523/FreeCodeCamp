Swami Vivekanand

The man who sent the message of Universal Brotherhood to the world

Here is a timeline of Swami Vivekanand's life:

  • 1863

    Vivekanand was born as Narendranath Datta in Calcutta, the then capital of British India, during the Makar Sakranti festival.

  • 1871

    At the age of eight, Narendranath enrolled at Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar's Metropolitan Institution, Calcutta

  • 1879

    Being the only student to recieve first-division maks, he then took admission in Presidency College, Calcutta

  • 1881-1884

    During this period,he passed the Fine Arts examination and obtained his Bachelor of Arts degree.

    Unfortunately, his father's sudden death in 1884 left him and his family's financial condition in dire straits, leading him to question about God's existence

    He found solace in Ramakrishna, a Hindu mystic, whom he had met duing his college years, and accepted him as his 'Spiritual Guru'.

  • 1885

    Ramakrishna, suffering from throat cancer and on his death bed, made Narendra the successor of his monastic order which was later named as 'Ramakrishna Math'.

  • 1886

    On the eve of Christmas, along with his eight other brother monks, he took formal monastic vows and took on the name 'Swami Vivekanand'.

  • 1888-1883

    Leaving the monastery, he decided to pursue a life of a wandering monk. For five years, he travelled extensively all around India, observing the diverse religious traditions and social patterns

    His observations led him to develop sympathy for the suffering and poverty of the people, and resolved to uplift the nation.

  • 1893-1897

    Vivekanand started his journey to the West on May 31st 1893, and visited several cities in Japan, China and Canada en route to the United States, reaching Chicago on July 30th, 1893.

    After facing some troubles because of the unavailability of valid credentials from a bona fide organization, he attended the 'Parliament of Religions' in September, 1893.

    On 11 September, 1893, he came up on the stage, he stunned everyone while saying "My brothers and sisters of America". For this, he received a standing ovation from the audience. He, then described the principles of Vedanta, their spiritual significance, etc.

    He stayed around two and a half years in America itself and founded the Vedanta Society of New York. He also travelled to the United Kingdom to preach the philosophies, spiritualism, and principles of Vedanta.

  • 1897-1899

    Around 1897, he returned to India and reached Calcutta where he founded Ramakrishna Mission on 1 May, 1897 at Belur Math.

    The goals of the mission were based on Karma Yoga and its main objective was to serve the poor and suffering or disturbed population of the country.

    Several social services are also performed under this mission like establishing schools, colleges, and hospitals.

    Teachings of Vedanta were also provided through conferences, seminars, and workshops, rehabilitation work across the country.

  • 1899

    Despite declining health, Vivekananda left for the West for a second time in June 1899.

  • 1902

    On 4 July, 1902, he died while doing meditation while fulfilling his prophecy that he would not live forty years.
